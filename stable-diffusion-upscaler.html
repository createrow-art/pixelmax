Frequently Asked Questions

Everything SD users ask about PixelMax.

How does PixelMax compare to SD's built-in hires.fix?

Hires.fix in Automatic1111 re-runs the diffusion process at higher resolution, which can change your image's composition. PixelMax uses Real-ESRGAN post-processing — it upscales exactly what you generated without altering the content. It's also dramatically simpler to use.

Do I need a GPU to use PixelMax?

No. PixelMax runs entirely in the cloud. You just need a web browser. This is especially useful for SD users on laptops or machines with limited VRAM who can't run local upscaling models.

What resolution does Stable Diffusion output?

SD 1.5 defaults to 512×512, SDXL outputs at 1024×1024, and custom workflows vary. All of these are too small for quality printing at 300 DPI. PixelMax 4× upscale takes any SD output to print-ready resolution.

How much does PixelMax cost?

PixelMax is $1 per upscaled image. No subscription, no software license. Compare to Topaz Gigapixel at $199/year or running your own ESRGAN setup with GPU costs.

Can I batch upscale multiple SD images?

Yes. PixelMax supports batch processing — upload multiple images and upscale them all in parallel. Perfect for SD users generating variations who need all outputs at high resolution.
